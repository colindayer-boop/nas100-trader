# Findings

## Summary of Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen (2012) — "Time Series Momentum"

**Publication:** *Journal of Financial Economics*, Vol 104, Issue 2, pp. 228–250.

**Core finding:** The paper documents **time-series momentum (TSMOM)** — a phenomenon where an asset's own past return predicts its future return in the same direction. If an asset is up over the past 12 months, it tends to continue up; if down, it tends to continue down. This is distinct from cross-sectional momentum (which ranks assets relative to each other).

### Data and scope

- **58 instruments** across 4 asset classes: 24 commodity futures, 12 equity index futures, 9 bond futures, 6 currency forwards, plus 9 additional equity indices for robustness.
- **Sample period:** 1985–2009 (25 years), extended back to 1800 for cotton, wheat, and corn in some tests.
- Data sourced from Bloomberg, CRB, Datastream, and exchange archives.

### Methodology

- **Signal:** Sign of cumulative return over a lookback window (1, 3, 6, 12, 24, 36, 48, 60 months). The canonical variant uses **12-month (252-trading-day) lookback**.
- **Position sizing:** Volatility-scaled — each position is sized to target a constant ex-ante volatility (using past 60-day realised volatility), producing a 1% monthly vol target per market.
- **Rebalancing:** Monthly.
- **Portfolio:** Equal-weight, fully collateralised, combining all 58 markets.
- **Execution:** Lagged by one observation period (signals at month-end, trades at next available price).

### Key results

| Metric | TSMOM portfolio (58 markets) |
|---|---|
| Annualised return | ~12.1% |
| Annualised volatility | ~10.5% |
| Sharpe ratio | **1.15** (combined) |
| Individual-market average Sharpe | ~0.41 |
| Max drawdown | Moderate; strong during crisis periods |

- Positive and statistically significant Sharpe ratios in **every individual market** (except 3 of 58).
- TSMOM subsumes **cross-sectional momentum**: when TSMOM is present, cross-sectional momentum alpha drops substantially.
- **Crisis alpha:** TSMOM generated large positive returns during 2008 (long bonds, short equities/credit), suggesting a tail-hedge property.
- Robust across lookback windows from 1 to 60 months, though 12-month is the strongest single horizon.
- Combining multiple lookback windows (1, 3, 12 months) further improves the Sharpe to ~1.8.

### Why it works (authors' interpretation)

- **Behavioural:** Underreaction to news (anchoring, disposition effect), followed by delayed overreaction (herding).
- **Institutional frictions:** Risk management systems, stop-loss cascades, and hedging demands create serial correlation.
- **Not explained by:** Market risk premium, value, or carry. TSMOM is distinct from these factors.
- **Post-publication concern:** Like many anomalies, there is evidence of partial decay after 2009 (CTA industry crowding), though the diversification benefit across 58 markets remains.

# Risks

- **CFD financing is the binding constraint.** The paper's results assume institutional futures where the carry cost of rolling is negligible. On the repo's Pepperstone/MT5 CFD venue, daily financing on gross notional (~3 bps/day ≈ 7.5%/yr) eliminates the edge entirely. This is a structural, non-fixable economic barrier.
- **Drawdown exceeds prop-firm gates.** Even on the unfinanced ETF account, the −36.6% MaxDD fails the repo's own −35% OOS drawdown criterion. The 2008 tail-hedge property helps in aggregate but individual instruments (especially commodities) show deep drawdowns.
- **Post-publication decay (2010–present).** The paper's sample ends 2009. The repo's 2004–2026 ETF backtest captures both pre- and post-publication data; the OOS Sharpe of 0.39 (vs the paper's 1.15) is consistent with meaningful decay as the CTA industry adopted and crowded the signal.
- **Concentration risk.** The repo tested 8 ETFs vs the paper's 58 markets. Diversification benefit is substantially reduced, which may explain part of the Sharpe gap (the paper's per-market average Sharpe is ~0.41, close to the repo's 0.45).
- **Paper stub remains `unread`.** The paper note at `research/papers/moskowitz-ooi-pedersen-2012-time-series-momentum.md` has an empty claim field, unchecked assessment checklist, and no verdict. The experiment was run without the paper's formal review being completed.
